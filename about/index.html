About

To every tired heart still choosing to care — these letters are for you.

I am a leader and director. For more than fifteen years I have led teams and operations across more than twenty countries, in crisis response, organizational design, and the human cost of doing important work. I have served with international NGOs, United Nations agencies, the Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement, and donor institutions across the Middle East, Africa, Asia, and Europe.

I write and speak about leadership, about care under pressure, and about the slow practice of staying human in work that rarely rewards it. I work in Arabic and English. I believe excellence is a practice, and that care is a choice.

I wrote these letters by candlelight, one each evening, mostly between cities, mostly after dark. They began as reflections I kept for myself. For a while I meant to gather them into a book. Then I changed my mind. They felt more honest here, free and open, for whoever needs them tonight. They are not a product. They are letters to a friend. That friend is you.

I did write a book once, Quantum Humanitarian, about what the work asks of us and what it leaves behind. I have also published research, and I keep a fuller record of the work elsewhere.
